ðŸ”¹ Project Overview

This project is a real-time water simulation built using C++ and OpenGL as part of my final assignment for CS3388 (Computer Graphics). The simulation leverages advanced rendering techniques such as tessellation shaders, geometry shaders, and displacement maps to bring a dynamic water surface to life.

At a high level, the application:

  • Generates a simple triangle mesh as a base.
  • Uses tessellation to increase mesh detail dynamically.
  • Applies Gerstner wave functions in the geometry shader to simulate realistic wave motion.
  • Implements a Phong-based lighting model with displacement and normal mapping for visual depth and texture.
  • Renders a visually convincing water patch with interactive camera controls and adjustable wave parameters.
